&nbsp;&nbsp;Quick Look GPX Files in macOS Finder.

**gjGPXPreview** is a lightweight macOS QuickLook extension that lets you preview GPX files directly in Finder — no app to open, no import needed. Just select a _.gpx_ file and press Space.

**Map View**  
See your track or route plotted on an interactive map with gradient-coloured polylines. Start and finish markers are placed automatically, and tracks are distinguished from routes with different colour schemes and line styles. Toggle waypoint markers on or off with a single click.

**Elevation Profile**  
View an elevation chart that plots altitude against distance for every track and route in the file. An area fill highlights the profile shape, and a summary bar shows minimum elevation, maximum elevation, total gain, and total loss at a glance. Multiple tracks and routes are drawn as separate colour-coded series with a legend.

**Statistics**  
Browse detailed statistics including total distance, elevation gain and loss, moving time, elapsed time, average and maximum speed, start and end times, and point count. A metadata section shows the file name, description, author, creation date, keywords, and any embedded links.

**Imperial and Metric**  
Switch between miles and kilometres for distance, and between feet and metres for elevation, using the quick-stat chips in the header. Your choice is reflected across the map header, elevation chart, and statistics view.

**Multiple Tracks and Routes**  
Files containing more than one track or route are fully supported. Each is drawn with its own colour on the map and as a separate series on the elevation chart, with per-track start and finish markers.

**Features:**
- Interactive map with gradient polylines and start/finish markers
- Toggleable waypoint markers
- Elevation profile chart with area fill and summary statistics
- Detailed statistics with distance, elevation, speed, and timing
- Metadata display including author, links, and keywords
- Switch between imperial and metric units
- Multi-track and multi-route support
- Automatic activity icon detection (running, cycling, hiking, and more)
- Native macOS app built with SwiftUI
